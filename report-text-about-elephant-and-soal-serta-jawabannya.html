16. tolong jawab secepatnyawrite down five(5) factual things about deer, monkey,elephant, tiger, bear, and zoo kepper


5 actual things about:

- deer : 
1. Deer are part of the Cervidae family that include moose, reindeer, elk and other species.
2. Deer are found all over the world, with the exception of Australia and Antarctica.
3. Deer are found in many different ecosystems. They live in wetlands, deciduous forests, grasslands, rain forests, arid scrublands and mountains
4. Deer are herbivores; they only eat vegetation.
5. Most deer are active throughout the day, though their most active times are during sunrise and dusk.

- monkey :
1. To communicate, monkeys use facial expressions, vocalizations and body movements.
2. Group of monkeys is called a troop.
3. Trees, grasslands, mountains, forests and high plains are the most common places where monkeys live.
4. Most of the monkeys eat both plants and animals, some monkeys also eat dirt.
5.  It is easy to distinguish monkeys from apes.  Monkeys have tails, apes don’t.

- elephant :
1. It is biggest animal
2. It has trunk
3. It is herbivore
4. Elephants are extremely intelligent animals and have memories that span many years.
5. Elephants receive the messages through the sensitive skin on their feet and trunks.

- tiger :
1. Tigers mainly eat ambar deer, wild pigs, water buffalo and antelope
2. Tigers occupy a variety of habitats from tropical forests, evergreen forests, woodlands and mangrove swamps to grasslands, savannahand rocky country.
3. They are mostly nocturnal (more active at night)
4. They are also very good swimmers and have been known to kill prey while swimming.
5. Tigers essentially live solitary lives, except during mating season and when females bear young.

- bear
1. Bears are extraordinarily intelligent animals. They have far superior navigation skills to humans; excellent memories; large brain to body ratio; and use tools in various contexts from play to hunting.
2. Bears grieve deeply for others. Cubs are known to moan and cry when separated from their mothers.  This can go on for weeks if their mothers are killed by hunters.
3. Bears have excellent senses of smell, sight and hearing. They can smell food, cubs, a mate or predators from miles away. Their great eyesight allows them to detect when fruits are ripe.
4. Bears care deeply about family members. They will risk their lives and even fight to the death in order to save a cub or sibling from danger.
5. Bears were often honoured in the cultures of many early civilisations. They were seen as a symbol of power, strength and love.

- zoo keeper :
1. Majority of zookeepers are required to have a degree associated with animals.
2. Zookeepers work on all Holidays – animals always need cared for!
3. Zookeepers work in all weather – rain, scorching temperatures, snow, wind, ice, etc.
4. Most zookeepers have moved far from home for a zookeeping job at some point in their life. 
5. Zookeepers will often check for their radios and zoo keys even when they are in civilian clothes and not at the zoo!
