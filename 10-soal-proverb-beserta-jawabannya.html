Don’t count your chickens before they’re hatched. Jangan pernah terlalu yakin bahwa sesuatu akan berjalan sesuai dengan keinginan anda.

Don’t dig your grave with our own knife and fork. Janganlah melakukan sesuatu hal yang membuat dirimu menderita.

Don’t judge a book by its cover. Janganlah anda menilai seseorang hanya dari penampilannya saja.

Don’t bark if you can’t bite. Jangan ‘ngotot’ jika tidak mempunyai alasan yang kuat.

Meanwhile, a riddle is a basically a statement or query about something to be guessed by others. It can be for fun or for meaningful one. Riddles can be made on your own if you're clever enough to create one.

Contoh Proverb:

A bad workman always blames his tools. Artinya: Seseorang menyalahkan kualitas alat atau faktor eksternal ketika mereka mengerjakan sesuatu dengan payah. A bird in hand is worth two in the bush. Artinya: Orang yang tampak menyeramkan jarang menyakiti seseorang.

Contoh Riddle:

What gets wetter and wetter the more it dries? (Answer: A towel)I have a tail, and I have a head, but I have no body. I am NOT a snake. What am I? (Answer: A coin)How many months have 28 days? (Answer: All of them)What word becomes shorter when you add letters to it? (Answer: "Short")What word begins with a T, ends with a T, and has a T in it? (Answer: A teapot)

Pembahasan

Proverb atau peribahasa adalah kata - kata bijak yang berisi nasihat tentang kehidupan. Bentuknya bisa kumpulan - kumpulan kata yang punya susunan dan arti tertentu yang memberikan nasihat positif.

Language feature:

Menggunakan bahasa konotasi.

Tipe proverb:

Sebagai warning (peringatan) -- Don't count your chickens before they are hatchedSebagai nasihat -- Never judge the book by its cover.Sebagai pesan moral -- Look before you leap.

Grammatical structure of English proverb:

Imperative negative -- Don't flog a dead horse.Imperative positive -- Look before you leap. Prallel phrase -- Garbage in, Garbage out. Declarative sentence -- Birds of feather flock together.
