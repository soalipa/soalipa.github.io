Once upon a time, in the heart of a dense forest in Indonesia, lived a clever little mouse deer named Kancil. Kancil was known for its wit and cunning. One day, as Kancil was walking by a river, he noticed a crocodile basking in the sun.

The crocodile saw Kancil and thought, "What a delicious meal this little creature would make!" He decided to approach Kancil and strike up a conversation.

"Hello, Kancil," said the crocodile with a sly smile. "Would you like to come closer and chat with me?"

Kancil, always quick on his feet, sensed danger but didn't show it. He replied, "Of course, dear crocodile. But how can I come over without getting wet? I can't swim, you know."

The crocodile, eager to catch Kancil, thought for a moment and then suggested, "I can carry you on my back. You won't get wet, and we can chat in the middle of the river."

Kancil agreed to the idea but had a clever plan in mind. He hopped onto the crocodile's back, and as they reached the middle of the river, Kancil asked, "Crocodile, do you know how to swim?"

The crocodile, surprised by the question, replied, "Of course, I'm a great swimmer!"

Kancil then asked, "Can you dive underwater with me on your back?"

The crocodile, not wanting to reveal his inability to swim properly, answered, "Of course, I can do that."

As they dove underwater, Kancil could feel the crocodile struggling to stay afloat. He knew the crocodile's weakness had been exposed. Kancil quickly leaped off the crocodile's back and swam to the riverbank, leaving the crocodile behind.

The crocodile realized he had been outsmarted by Kancil and shouted, "Kancil, you are indeed clever. I won't try to catch you again."

Kancil, with a mischievous grin, replied, "Remember, dear crocodile, it's not wise to mess with someone smarter than you."

From that day on, the crocodile never dared to harm Kancil, knowing that the tiny creature's intelligence was a force to be reckoned with.

And so, the clever Kancil once again proved that intelligence can triumph over strength.
