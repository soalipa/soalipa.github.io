ONE, EACH, DAN EVERY

Kata one, each, dan every digunakan untuk menunjukan jumlah tunggal sebuah kata benda dalam bahasa Inggris. One, each dan every diikuti oleh singular count nouns (kata benda tunggal yang bisa dihitung) bukan kata benda jamak atau kata benda yang tidak bisa dihitung.

Contoh kalimat ? “Where is a suitable place for a picnic?”

Contoh kalimat ? “How much does that book cost?”

Dalam bahasa inggris, makna dan pesan moral dari lagu First Love - Nikka Costa adalah sebagai berikut:

The song "First Love" by Nikka Costa is about a girl experiencing her first love. Sadly, first love doesn't always have a happy ending. In this song's case, the love the girl is experiencing isn't returned because the boy thinks that she's too young.

Pembahasan

"First Love" adalah lagu yang terdapat dalam album berjudul sama yang dirilis pada tahun 1983. Nikki Costa sendiri merupakan seorang penyanyi dan penulis lagu yang lahir di Tokyo pada tanggal 4 Juni 1972.

coz I'm feeling my first love

Will he ever notice me? Could he ever fall?

Will he ever find a way and answer to my pray?

Secara garis besar, lagu ini menceritakan tentang seorang gadis kecil yang tengah mengalami cinta pertamanya. Sayangnya, lelaki yang ia sukai merasa bahwa ia terlalu muda. Meskipun begitu, si gadis tak putus asa untuk memperjuangkan perasaan tersebut.

Pesan yang terkandung dalam lagu First Love ini adalah bahwa kisah cinta, termasuk cinta pertama, tidak selalu memiliki akhir yang bahagia.

40. cerita singkat the first well pakai bahasa inggris dan mudah dihafal


THE FIRST WELL

There once was a small kingdom around a lake. One very hot summer, it did
not rain and the lake dried up. People grew anxious and went to the King. “It
has not rained for so long. Our fields are barren!” said the farmers. “There
are no fish to catch. How shall we earn a living?” asked the fishermen. “Save
us from disaster, good King,” urged the women, as their children cried with
thirst.
The King sent his four generals in all directions to look for water. The first
general went east, towards the sunrise; the second went south, to the dust
and heat; the third went west, where the sun sets; and the fourth followed
the North Star.
They searched day and night, night and day; high and low they searched
everywhere, but in vain. Three of the generals returned, empty-handed. But
the general who had gone north was determined not to fail his King. At last,
he reached a cold mountain village.
As he sat at the foot of the mountain, an old woman came by and sat next
to him. The general pointed at the horizon and said, “I belong to a beautiful
kingdom, where it has not rained for a whole year. Can you help me find
water?”

The woman motioned the general to follow her up the mountain and into a
cave. “We have no water in our country either,” she said. Then, pointing to
the icicles in the cave, she continued: “We call this ice. Take some, and your
kingdom will never go thirsty again.” The general broke off a huge piece,
